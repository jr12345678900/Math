Voronoi Diagrams & Sinusoidal Functions

📚 The Interactive Cheat Sheet

1. Voronoi Diagrams: A Voronoi diagram divides a space into regions (cells) based on the closest distance to a specific point (a "site"). The boundaries (edges) between these regions are always the of the line segment connecting two sites.
• Intersection points where three boundaries meet are called . A vertex is equidistant from all three surrounding sites.
2. Sinusoidal Models \( y = a \sin(b(x-c)) + d \):
• The Principal Axis (\(d\)) is the center line. It is found by calculating the of the Maximum and Minimum values.
• The Amplitude (\(a\)) is the distance from the center to the peak. You find it by doing: \( (Max - Min) \) divided by .
• The Period is how long it takes to repeat one full wave. The formula to find \(b\) is: \( b = \frac{360^\circ}{\text{Period}} \).

🌊 Guided Practice 2: The Tidal Wave

The depth of water, \( d \), in a harbor (in meters) over a 24-hour period is modeled by the function:

\( d(t) = a \sin(b(t - c)) + d \)

At high tide, the water depth is 14 meters. At low tide, the depth is 4 meters. The time between one high tide and the very next high tide is exactly 12 hours.
Part A: The Principal Axis (\(d\))
The principal axis is the average water level (the line right in the middle of the wave).
\( d = \frac{\text{Max} + \text{Min}}{2} \)
\( d = \frac{14 + 4}{2} = \) meters.
Part B: The Amplitude (\(a\))
The amplitude is the distance the wave rises above the middle line.
\( a = \frac{\text{Max} - \text{Min}}{2} \)
\( a = \frac{14 - 4}{2} = \) meters.
Part C: The Period and \(b\) Value
The problem states that the time between high tides is 12 hours. Therefore, the Period is hours.
To find the mathematical value for \(b\) to put into the equation, we use the formula:
\( b = \frac{360^\circ}{\text{Period}} \)
\( b = \frac{360^\circ}{12} = \) \(^\circ\).
