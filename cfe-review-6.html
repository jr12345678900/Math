Voronoi Diagrams & Sinusoidal Functions

📚 The Interactive Cheat Sheet

1. Voronoi Diagrams: A Voronoi diagram divides a space into regions (cells) based on the closest distance to a specific point (a "site"). The boundaries (edges) between these regions are always the of the line segment connecting two sites.
• Intersection points where three boundaries meet are called . A vertex is equidistant from all three surrounding sites.
2. Sinusoidal Models \( y = a \sin(bx) + d \):
• The Principal Axis (\(d\)) is the center line. It is found by calculating the of the Maximum and Minimum values.
• The Amplitude (\(a\)) is the distance from the center to the peak. You find it by doing: \( \frac{\text{Max} - \text{Min}}{2} \).
• The Period is how long it takes to repeat one full wave. The formula to find the \(b\) value is: \( b = \frac{360^\circ}{\text{Period}} \).
3. Choosing the Trig Function:
• If the graph starts at the middle and goes UP, use Positive Sine.
• If the graph starts at the Maximum Peak and goes DOWN, use Positive Cosine.
